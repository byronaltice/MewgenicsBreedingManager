DIRECTION 48 NAME EXTRACTION BUG
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Direction 48 used a backward-scan heuristic to find child names in
  PlaceObject2 tags. This produced 'Nmouth' instead of 'mouth' at depth=66.
  'Nmouth' is not in the ANCHOR_SET so Direction 48 never recorded 'mouth'.
  This script's forward-parsing correctly extracts 'mouth' at depth=66.
  Effect on prior Direction 48 report: 'mouth' was never present in any
  of Dir48's results, but this is a display bug only. The frames checked
  in Direction 48 (99/304/319) did not differ in the other 7 named anchors,
  so the 'identical' conclusion for frame 99 vs 304 was correct for those.
  However: Direction 48 did NOT check frames 98/303/318 (headShape-1).

  Root cause of discrepancy with Direction 48:
    Direction 48 checked frames 99/304/319 (headShape directly).
    At those frames, re-placement tags (fn=304, fn=319) ARE committed,
    restoring the removed anchors. Result: sets appeared identical.
    Direction 54 confirmed the actual code uses headShape-1.
    At frames 303/318 (headShape-1), the removal tags are committed
    but the re-placement tags are NOT yet committed.
    Result: reye absent for Whommie, lear+rear absent for Bud.

  This resolves the investigation for both Whommie and Bud:
    The CatHeadPlacements SWF, under headShape-1 frame seeking,
    produces missing anchor names that cause FUN_140734760 to
    leave CatPart+0x18=0 for the corresponding head part slots.
    The renderer then emits 0xFFFFFFFE for those slots.
    The parser detects 0xFFFFFFFE as a birth defect.
    The entire missing-defect chain is now explained end-to-end.

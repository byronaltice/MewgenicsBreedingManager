VERDICT
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Option (b) + (c): The game saves a "random_seed" in the SQLite "properties"
table. At load time, FUN_140230750 reads it and seeds TLS+0x178. Subsequent
RNG-driven calls to FUN_1400ca4a0 (in GlobalProgressionData::ComputeSave-
FilePercentage and in room/interaction update functions) deterministically
apply birth_defect mutations to cat body-part slots. The CatPart+0x18 flag
that drives the 0xFFFFFFFE display is set as a side effect of this RNG-based
mutation application, not from any per-cat blob field.

This means birth defects for Whommie/Bud are NOT stored per-cat in the blob.
They are reconstructed at load time from the global random_seed, the global
birth_defect candidate tables, and the per-cat state at the time of mutation
application (body-part container initialized from T-array, then RNG-driven
defects layered on top).
